Story about honeybees

A young honeybee wakes up, eager to work for its large family in a dark, bustling hive. It starts by tending to baby bees with royal jelly and building wax cells, before taking on the risky, rewarding job of foraging.

The bee drinks nectar from flowers, helps pollinate, and performs a waggle dance to show sisters where to find food, working tirelessly to create sweet honey for the colony.

Fun Facts:
Every bee is female.

Also bees are also in the same order as ants (Hymenoptera) but ants are a sister group to the group Apocrita that contains bees.

Glossery:

Order means Group.
